HPD Vacate Order Summary

Source: NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD). A vacate order is HPD's own determination that a building, or part of one, was dangerous enough that residents had to leave -- a stronger displacement signal than a citation, since it represents forced displacement rather than a violation that may or may not be remediated.

Editorial Note

Unlike routine violation-count thresholds elsewhere in this ruleset (e.g. PHC-001's three-or-more Class C requirement), VA-001 requires no minimum count above one: a vacate order already represents HPD's own severe displacement determination, not a citation that may or may not be remediated. This rule does not apply a recency window -- a long-rescinded historical order and a currently active one both satisfy the base verdict, distinguished only by the active/recurring sub-signals shown above.
