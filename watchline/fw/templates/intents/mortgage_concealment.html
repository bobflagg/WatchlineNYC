Purchase-Money Financing Comparison

Compares the grantee named on this building's most recent ACRIS deed transfer against the mortgagor named on the mortgage recorded nearest to that closing (-14 to +60 days). Under normal purchase-money financing the buyer of record and the borrower of record are the same party.

Calibration Note

Verified against live evidentiary data: of matched purchase-money deed/ mortgage pairs (grantee vs. mortgagor within a -14 to +60 day window), 85.1% show an exact name-token match, 12.0% show partial overlap, and only 2.9% show zero overlap — the population this rule flags. This is a materially cleaner signal than OwnershipNameDiscrepancy (71% mismatch rate) because both names here come from the same ACRIS self-reporting convention on the same closing package, not two independently-sourced datasets. Corporate and individual names are both evaluated — an LLC taking title while an unrelated individual finances the purchase is itself part of the flagged pattern, not excluded noise. This rule evaluates only the building's most recent deed transfer, not its full transaction history.
