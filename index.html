The public record data for 122 West 97th Street in Manhattan suggests this building is deteriorating. Here is what the evidence shows and how that conclusion was reached.

The building has 418 residential units, of which 254 are rent-stabilized. The data analyzed comes from HPD, the New York City agency that tracks housing maintenance violations.

The question of whether a building is getting worse is answered here using Rule DT-001, the Watchline Deterioration Trajectory rule. To understand how it works, some background is helpful. A Class C violation is the most serious category of housing code violation that HPD issues. It covers conditions that are immediately hazardous to health and safety, such as no heat, no hot water, vermin infestations, and dangerous structural conditions. The rule looks at five full calendar years of Class C violation data and asks two questions.

Signal A asks whether violations are being issued more frequently in recent years than in earlier years. Signal B asks whether those violations are being resolved less reliably in recent years than in earlier years. The threshold statement for Rule DT-001 reads verbatim: "A building satisfies Rule DT-001 (Deterioration Trajectory) if two signals are both present over the most recent 5 full calendar years: (A) average annual Class C violation issuance is higher in the most recent 2 years than in the earliest 2 years of the window; and (B) the resolution rate of Class C violations that have been open more than 180 days is lower in the most recent 2 years than in the earliest 2 years. Both signals must be satisfied."

Both signals are satisfied here.

For Signal A, looking at the earliest two years in the window, 2021 and 2022, the building averaged 19.5 Class C violations issued per year. In the most recent two full years, 2024 and 2025, that average rose to 71.5 violations per year. That is a more than threefold increase in annual issuance.

For Signal B, in 2021 and 2022 the average resolution rate for eligible violations was approximately 59.6 percent. In 2024 and 2025 that rate fell to approximately 33.7 percent. Fewer than one in three eligible violations is now being resolved in the recent period, compared to roughly three in five in the earlier period.

The year 2025 shows a particularly striking figure: 121 violations issued in a single year for a building of this size. However, 2025 is a partial year in progress, and its resolution rate of about 54 percent should be treated as an early signal only. The rule conclusion for 2025 is not applied in isolation; it contributes to the two-year average alongside 2024.

The interpretive status of this finding is Inferred. That means the conclusion follows logically from the data but has not been confirmed by a regulator or court. The rule itself is a Watchline editorial tool, not a statutory or regulatory definition. It is designed to flag patterns worth investigation, not to make legal findings.

No conclusion is drawn here about why conditions have changed or about the intentions of anyone responsible for the building.

Cypher Query Executed

This is the exact query run against the Watchline knowledge graph to retrieve the data behind this answer.

MATCH (b:Building {bbl: $bbl}) MATCH (b)-[:HAS_EVENT]->(e:Event {source_name: 'HPD', event_type: 'Violation', violation_class: 'C'}) WHERE e.open_date IS NOT NULL WITH b, e, e.open_date.year AS issue_year, duration.inDays(e.open_date, date()).days AS age_days, CASE WHEN e.status = 'Closed' AND e.current_status_date IS NOT NULL THEN duration.inDays(e.open_date, e.current_status_date).days ELSE null END AS days_to_resolve WITH b, collect(CASE WHEN issue_year >= 2021 AND issue_year <= 2025 THEN {year: issue_year, age: age_days, resolved: e.status = 'Closed', days_to_resolve: days_to_resolve} END) AS window_events, collect(CASE WHEN issue_year = 2026 THEN {age: age_days, resolved: e.status = 'Closed'} END) AS current_year_events UNWIND window_events AS we WITH b, current_year_events, we WHERE we IS NOT NULL WITH b, current_year_events, we.year AS yr, count(*) AS issued, sum(CASE WHEN we.age > 180 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS eligible, sum(CASE WHEN we.age > 180 AND we.resolved THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS resolved_of_eligible WITH b, current_year_events, collect({ year: yr, issued: issued, eligible: eligible, resolved_of_eligible: resolved_of_eligible, resolution_rate: CASE WHEN eligible > 0 THEN toFloat(resolved_of_eligible) / eligible ELSE null END }) AS yearly, size([x IN current_year_events WHERE x IS NOT NULL]) AS cy_issued, size([x IN current_year_events WHERE x IS NOT NULL AND x.age > 180]) AS cy_over_180 RETURN b.bbl AS bbl, b.address AS address, b.borough AS borough, b.residential_units AS residential_units, yearly AS annual_trajectory, cy_issued AS current_year_issued, cy_over_180 AS current_year_over_180
